05, When things go off-script

Six moments that aren't the happy path. Each one, handled.

Card got declined
Their card doesn't work this month.
Stripe retries 4 times in a week. Emails them. We show a yellow banner. No surprise lockout.
They click cancel
Wants out.
Stays in until the month they paid for ends. After that, a one-click resubscribe button.
Stripe is slow to tell us
Stripe took the money but their text to us is delayed.
We also check Stripe directly. The door still opens.
They come back later
Cancelled three months ago. Wants back in.
Stripe remembers them. New subscription on the same account. Instant access.
Primrose issues a refund
She refunds someone from Stripe.
Access ends automatically. No manual cleanup.
Same person pays twice
Existing member accidentally checks out again.
Stripe blocks the duplicate. We show "you're already in."
06, Things we won't let break

Eight ways a platform like this usually breaks. We close every one.

Paid but locked out
Stripe took the money, our system hasn't caught up.
We check both the receipt and Stripe. Door opens either way.
Cancel timing wrong
Site says locked, Stripe says still in. Or vice versa.
Stripe is always the truth. We never guess.
Silent failed payment
Card dies mid-subscription, no one notices.
Yellow banner everywhere until they fix it. Stripe emails them too.
Video in wrong place
She publishes it but it shows in the wrong pillar.
One source of truth. The library reads from one place.
Admin oops
She publishes a draft by accident. Or deletes the wrong video.
Clear draft/live labels. "Are you sure?" before deletes.
Videos look different
Thumbnails vary. Long titles break the layout.
Template forces consistency. Her content always fits the design.
Logged out mid-video
Session expires, they start over.
Long sessions. Player remembers where they paused.
She gets stranded
Something feels off, she doesn't know what to do.
Walkthrough video. 14 days of free fixes after launch.

09, Keeping the library safe

Three layers between her library and the open internet.

The three layers
Vimeo domain lock
Video plays only on her site. Hidden everywhere else on the web.
Kills URL sharing outside the platform.
Stripe check every page
Each video load asks Stripe live: "is this person still paying?"
Cancelled members are locked out instantly.
Browser friction
Right-click off. Download button hidden. No share widget on the player.
Stops casual click-to-download.
The three scenarios you asked about
"They share the URL"
Member sends a video link to a friend.
URL won't play anywhere except her domain. Friend gets a private-video error.
"They pay, download, cancel"
Subscribe for one month, grab everything, leave.
No bulk download UI. Player blocks downloads. After cancel, every page kicks them out.
"They share their login"
One account, multiple people logging in.
Magic-link login goes to their email. Hard to share without sharing the inbox.
If leaks ever become a real problem: the upgrade lane is Cloudflare Stream with signed tokens (URLs that expire in minutes and only work for the logged-in user), and above that, full DRM. Neither is needed at Phase 1.
