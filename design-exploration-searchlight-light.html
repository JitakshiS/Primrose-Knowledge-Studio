For Jitakshi · daylight vs night

Same experience, two worlds.

What the light world changes

  • The torch becomes a reading lens: the truths sit blurred like unread fine print until your pointer makes them crisp, with highlighter marks on the phrases that matter. Same interaction, and arguably a truer metaphor for legal content.
  • The dawn arc inverts: the page opens in a warm lamp-lit vignette and brightens to clean daylight as you scroll. The clock still advances from 2:47 to 7:12.
  • The video player stays a dark room inside the light page, which is honest: video gets watched in the dark, everything around it lives in daylight.
  • Trust reads higher: light + hairline borders + soft shadows is the visual language of banks and institutions, which suits a lawyer's product.

What the night version still does better

  • Feed-stopping power: a dark site screenshot punches harder between bright TikTok and IG posts.
  • Atmosphere: glow and grain carry more drama on black. The light version trades cinema for credibility.
  • The 2:47 AM story is more literal at night. In daylight the lamp conceit carries it, but the night version needs no explanation.
  • A third option exists: ship the light version as the Porch and use the night world inside the member area, where late-night usage is real. The two variants share every token except the surface palette.
