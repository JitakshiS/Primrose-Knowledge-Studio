Visual Direction · v2

Two wider swings — and Channel still on the table.

Studio carried too much DNA from the tax site. So we go genuinely different. Two new directions below: one dark and cinematic, one built on the pillars as a five-colour brand system. Channel from v1 stays in the conversation — you said you preferred it, so it's the baseline these two have to beat. Pick one of the three at the end, I scaffold around it the moment the deposit lands.

What stays out
Anything warm-cream-editorial. The tax site already owns that territory and we don't want Knowledge Studio reading like a sibling. No Bricolage, no Playfair, no muted earth palettes.
What stays in
The non-negotiables hold. 16px rounded, no UI serifs, photography of Primrose anchors trust, monospace details where they add flavour, audience-first not client-first.
Direction C · v2

LATE NIGHT

A focused, premium reading room — trust through gravity and atmosphere.

Dark backgrounds carry premium and seriousness in a way few light brands can. Knowledge Studio's audience is anxious-at-11pm Googling their rights — Late Night meets them where they are. Warm cream type sits against deep ink, sodium amber punches the CTAs and accent moments, and photography of Primrose becomes the warm anchor against the dark — like the lamp in a study. Reads cinematic, premium, and unmistakably designed. Closest energy: Apple TV+ subscription pages meets Substack Notes meets a premium creator's reading platform.

Direction D · v2

PILLAR
STACK.

Five pillars, five confident colours — the framework is the identity.

Most education platforms decorate their categories with stock icons. Pillar Stack does the opposite — the five categories are the brand. Each pillar gets a confident, curated colour. Together they form a system you remember after one visit. Off-white paper and near-black ink keep the colours sharp. The brand mark is a stack of five dots. The hero leans on the framework, not on photography. Reads like a small, careful publishing house that figured out how to package an OS. Closest energy: Stripe's colour-block marketing meets a modern indie magazine meets a confident brand identity.

My honest read

If you still like Channel after seeing these — go with Channel. It already clears the brief and you have conviction on it. If Late Night grabs you, it's the most distinctive of the three (dark education brands are rare) but it leans on photography, so it needs a great portrait of Primrose to land. If Pillar Stack grabs you, it's the most identity-led — the brand becomes the system itself, which is memorable and shareable, but it asks Primrose to commit to a confident colour world she may want to react to first.
