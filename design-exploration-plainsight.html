05

Straight answers.

Is this legal advice?

No. Everything in the library is legal information, not legal advice. It helps you understand your situation and know what to ask. It doesn't replace a lawyer when your situation is actively unfolding. Disclaimers are on every video.

Why $29 a month?

It's enough for Primrose to keep making videos and not enough to gatekeep this from the people who most need it. The library grows every month with new material. If you only use one video the whole year, it should save you more than a year of membership.

Is this for Canadians only?

The library is written from Canadian law. Most of the workplace and personal safety material applies broadly; the financial material is Canada-specific. If you are outside Canada, the principles still help, but the specifics may not match your jurisdiction.

What if the law changes after a video is published?

Every video carries a last-reviewed date, and videos get re-recorded when the law moves. If you ever spot something that looks out of date, there's a report link on every video page and Primrose reviews those within a business day.

Next time something happens, you'll know where to look.

For Jitakshi · the honest comparison

Plain Sight vs the current Porch.

This is a different philosophy, structure changed, sections changed, the argument changed. The current Porch says "this brand is confident." Plain Sight says "this product is trustworthy." Different bets.

What Plain Sight does differently

  • Shows the actual product in the hero (a working library preview) instead of brand mood.
  • Sells through a real scenario timeline ("A real Tuesday night") instead of abstract feature cards. The user flow IS the pitch.
  • Full catalogue visible before paying, with last-reviewed dates on legal content. Radical transparency as the conversion strategy.
  • Credentials structured like a record: bar status, practising since, response time. Verifiable claims over vibes.
  • Cancellation steps printed inside the pricing section. The exit on the label builds more trust than any testimonial.
  • One quiet accent; pillar colours demoted to small functional tags. Restraint reads as institutional confidence.

What it costs vs the current Porch

  • Less scroll-stopping in a TikTok feed. Quiet design converts warm traffic better than cold traffic.
  • The five-colour identity nearly disappears; screenshots are less recognisably "Primrose" at a glance.
  • The last-reviewed-date promise creates a real editorial obligation for Primrose. Powerful, but she has to commit to it.
  • Reads slightly older. Wealthsimple-calm rather than TikTok-native energy.
